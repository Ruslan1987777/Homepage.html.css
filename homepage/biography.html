Biography

Dr. Ruslan Baiazitov is a Steinway Educational Partner and an internationally acclaimed pianist, composer, and educator with a career that seamlessly blends performance excellence, pedagogical innovation, and creative composition. His recent album featuring 24 Etudes, Op. 10 and Op. 25 by Frédéric Chopin has garnered wide recognition and is available on Amazon, Apple Music, and Spotify. His ongoing project, 12 Transcendental Etudes by Franz Liszt, has received support from the prestigious MTNA Foundation Fund through a Teacher Enrichment Grant.

As a recording artist, Ruslan has also released a CD featuring compositions by Ferruccio Busoni and Rodion Shchedrin, showcasing his deep engagement with diverse and challenging repertoire. His original works, including Piano Pieces for Children, Op. 1 and Piano Pieces, Op. 2, have been published in Russia and featured as repertoire in competitions such as the First International Competition “In Harmony with the Piano” in Penza, where he served as chairman. His symphonic composition, Christmas Variations on “Silent Night,” was recorded at the Gnesin Russian Academy of Music under the baton of Vladimir Yatskevich.

Dr. Baiazitov’s distinguished performance career includes collaborations with conductors such as Johannes Wildner, Georg Mark, and Sergei Imashbaev, performing iconic works like Tchaikovsky’s Second Piano Concerto and Rachmaninoff’s Second Piano Concerto. He has premiered contemporary pieces, including Karlheinz Essl’s Sequitur XIII for extended piano and live electronics in Vienna, and participated in innovative projects like “Piandemia” in Houston. His competitive achievements span first prizes at the OJÄB Musik Competition in Vienna and top honors in Finland and Kazakhstan.

Since 2022, Dr. Baiazitov has served as an adjunct professor at San Jacinto College, where he has been recognized with a Certificate of Excellence for Outstanding Teaching. He teaches class piano, private piano, music appreciation, music theory, sight-singing, and ear training, and has also directed the string ensemble. His innovative teaching initiatives include the Halloween with Kawai Project, and his works were featured in the Winter Showcase at Allegro Pearland Academy of Music (2021). Ruslan has also served as a judge for the Galveston Music Club Federation Festival and the All Star Festival in Texas (2021).

As a composer, Ruslan’s music has reached international audiences. Steinway Artist Krassimira Jordan performed one of his pieces at the Washington International Piano Arts Council, further cementing his reputation as a versatile and influential figure in the world of classical music.

Ruslan’s education spans several renowned institutions, including the Kazan State Conservatory in Russia, the Musik und Kunst Privat Universität der Stadt Wien, and the Universität für Musik und Darstellende Kunst in Vienna, where he studied with Klaus Sticken and Thomas Kreuzberger. He holds a Doctor of Musical Arts degree from the Moores School of Music at the University of Houston, where he studied piano with Dr. Tali Morgulis and Professor Timothy Hester and symphonic conducting with Professor Franz Anton Krager.

Under his mentorship, Ruslan’s students excel in ABRSM piano and theory exams, consistently achieving distinction and merit grades. His contributions to music education also include the publication of the Reference Book for Group Piano, adopted into the curricula at the University of Houston and San Jacinto College.

Dr. Baiazitov continues to inspire audiences and students worldwide with his dedication to advancing the art of piano performance and education.
